# 30-day high-value action plan for Tim Hanewich’s online image

## Goal
Make his online presence communicate, within 30 seconds:

- who he is,
- what he’s best at,
- why he’s unusually credible,
- and where to go next.

The mission is not “be more impressive.”
The mission is: **make existing impressiveness legible.**

---

# Phase 1: Clarify the brand
## Days 1–3

### 1. Pick one primary positioning statement
He needs one sentence that becomes the anchor for his site, LinkedIn, bio, and talks.

Good formula:

**I help [audience] do [valuable outcome] using [specialty], with a background in [differentiator].**

Possible version:
**I build AI and Power Platform solutions for public sector organizations, with a maker’s background in robotics, autonomous systems, and open-source engineering.**

Shorter version:
**AI + Power Platform engineer for public sector, with deep maker and robotics chops.**

This should become the canonical description everywhere.

### 2. Choose 3 brand pillars
Everything on the internet should roll up into 3 buckets max.

Example:
- **AI and intelligent apps**
- **Power Platform / enterprise solution architecture**
- **Robotics, autonomous systems, and open-source engineering**

If something doesn’t fit one of those pillars, it becomes secondary.

### 3. Write a “why me” paragraph
A tight paragraph for website/about/LinkedIn.

Example structure:
- what he does,
- what makes him different,
- proof points,
- kind of problems he likes solving.

This becomes reusable copy across platforms.

---

# Phase 2: Fix the homepage
## Days 4–8

### 4. Rewrite the homepage hero section
The top of the website should instantly answer:
- Who are you?
- What do you do?
- Why should I care?

A strong homepage structure:

**Headline:**  
Build practical AI systems, enterprise apps, and autonomous machines.

**Subheadline:**  
I’m Tim Hanewich, an engineer focused on AI, Power Platform, and experimental robotics—building systems that are both useful in production and fun to push to the edge.

**Buttons:**  
- View Featured Work
- Read Writing
- GitHub
- Contact

No mystery. No scavenger hunt. No “welcome to my digital playground” vagueness.

### 5. Add a featured work section
Pick only 3–5 strongest projects.

Each one should include:
- title,
- one-sentence summary,
- why it matters,
- tech stack,
- outcome,
- link.

Good categories:
- one enterprise/public sector solution,
- one AI-related build,
- one robotics/autonomous project,
- one open-source or developer tool.

This is the single highest-value website improvement.

### 6. Add proof near the top
Put credibility signals above the fold or shortly below it:
- Microsoft role or affiliation,
- speaking appearances,
- open-source traction,
- articles/features,
- notable project stats.

Visitors should not have to dig for proof.

---

# Phase 3: Turn projects into case studies
## Days 9–14

### 7. Rewrite top projects as outcome-driven case studies
For each major project, use this format:

- **Problem**
- **Approach**
- **Tech used**
- **Key challenges**
- **Outcome**
- **What makes it interesting**

This is much stronger than just “here’s a thing I built.”

### 8. Add measurable outcomes wherever possible
Examples:
- reduced manual effort by X,
- enabled Y users,
- processed Z data,
- improved response time,
- deployed across N teams,
- integrated A with B.

Even if exact numbers can’t be shared, approximate impact is better than none.

### 9. Separate “professional work” from “experimental work”
Not because one is better than the other, but because each serves a different audience.

Suggested labels:
- **Production Solutions**
- **Experimental Builds**
- **Open Source**
- **Writing / Talks**

This preserves range without creating confusion.

---

# Phase 4: Clean up GitHub
## Days 15–18

### 10. Pin only the best repos
The pinned repos should be intentional, not random.

Choose repos that best support the brand pillars:
- one AI repo,
- one enterprise/developer tooling repo,
- one robotics/autonomy repo,
- one especially polished repo,
- one repo with traction or uniqueness,
- one repo that shows range but still fits the story.

### 11. Standardize README quality
Each important repo should have:
- a clear title,
- one-sentence summary,
- why it matters,
- setup steps,
- screenshots or diagrams if useful,
- roadmap or status,
- links back to personal site.

A polished README dramatically upgrades perceived professionalism.

### 12. Archive or de-emphasize weaker repos
Not everything needs front-stage visibility.
If some repos are unfinished, redundant, or low-signal, archive them or stop featuring them prominently.

The goal is not to look busy.
The goal is to look excellent.

---

# Phase 5: Fix LinkedIn and bio surfaces
## Days 19–21

### 13. Rewrite the LinkedIn headline
Avoid generic role-only branding.

Weak:
**Technical Specialist at Microsoft**

Better:
**AI and Power Platform Engineer | Public Sector Solutions | Robotics and Open-Source Builder**

The headline should combine day job + differentiator.

### 14. Rewrite the LinkedIn About section
Use this structure:
- opening identity statement,
- what kinds of systems he builds,
- what industries/problems he focuses on,
- differentiator,
- invitation to connect.

Keep it concrete and human.

### 15. Use one consistent bio everywhere
Prepare:
- 1-line bio
- 2-line bio
- 100-word bio

Use the same positioning across website, GitHub, talks, and profiles.

Consistency compounds.

---

# Phase 6: Own search results
## Days 22–24

### 16. Cross-link everything
Every major profile should point to the main site:
- GitHub
- LinkedIn
- speaker profiles
- article author pages
- other platforms

Everywhere should also use the same name formatting:
**Tim Hanewich**

This helps search engines connect the identity properly.

### 17. Improve SEO basics on the personal site
Make sure the site includes:
- clear page titles,
- meta descriptions,
- structured headings,
- Open Graph metadata,
- descriptive project page titles,
- a clean About page.

A homepage titled something vague wastes ranking power.

### 18. Publish an “About” page that is search-friendly
This page should explicitly include:
- full name,
- role,
- specialties,
- current focus,
- links to work,
- selected accomplishments.

That page is often one of the strongest search assets.

---

# Phase 7: Build authority, not just presence
## Days 25–30

### 19. Publish 2–3 high-quality essays
Not shallow updates. Actual useful thinking.

Good topics:
- lessons from building AI agents in the real world,
- where Power Platform works brilliantly and where it doesn’t,
- what enterprise software can learn from robotics,
- what building autonomous systems taught him about software architecture.

The goal is to sound like someone who has opinions earned through practice.

### 20. Create one flagship piece of content
This could be:
- “My favorite projects and what they taught me”
- “How I think about AI + Power Platform + autonomous systems”
- “A field guide to practical engineering across enterprise and experimental systems”

This becomes the best possible introduction for strangers.

### 21. Add a speaking/media page
Even if small now, this pays off later.
Include:
- talks,
- articles,
- podcasts,
- guest appearances,
- interview availability,
- topics he can speak on.

This subtly upgrades him from “person with projects” to “public expert.”

### 22. Collect 3 testimonials
Short quotes from colleagues, collaborators, or stakeholders can do a shocking amount of work.
Best if they speak to:
- technical depth,
- creativity,
- execution,
- communication.

---

# Highest ROI moves if he only does 5 things

If he ignores everything else, do these:

1. **Write one brutally clear positioning statement**
2. **Redesign homepage hero + featured work**
3. **Turn top 3 projects into case studies with outcomes**
4. **Curate and polish pinned GitHub repos**
5. **Standardize bio/headline across all profiles**

That alone would probably create a major improvement.

---

# Litmus test for every page
For each page/profile/repo, ask:

- Would a stranger know what I’m best at in 15 seconds?
- Is there proof near the top?
- Is this showing outcomes or just activity?
- Does this reinforce my main identity?
- Does this make me easier to trust, hire, invite, or collaborate with?

If not, it needs editing.

That’s the whole game.
